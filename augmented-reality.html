What is Augmented Reality (AR)?

AR is where you get to "project" or "Augment" a digital world onto the real world, this way you can interact with it and view it in different perspectives.

SEE OUR DEMOS

ARfrica ARKit Fashion

Looking into the future and how Augmented Reality can be utilized to make decision as well as visualize clothing, of course in this case the African heritage was the main idea where we got to fuse the Kitenge, Dashiki, Kenge and African Print in general. 

Turkana Boy ARKit Museum

The 2021 Kraal exhibition was an eye opener to the rich African heritage, during the Nairobi Museum tours, the Turkana Boy stood out, and this is the result.

Sogallo AR - ARKit Project

Sogallo is an artist based in Nairobi. He deals with a lot of line work and paint mediums. For this piece, the idea was to visualize his line art in 3D space, which we did in Augmented Reality using ARKit where you can actually walk around the artwork.

Kraal AR Museum Project

During the 2021 Kraal Exhibition at the national museum in Nairobi, Kraal the exhibitors and curators of the exhibition wanted a digital side of it all. So we got to work with Marvin Abwao on his art piece which was translated to AR for the public.

ARKit Periodic Table

Augment the peridodic table onto the real world and take a look at all the elements that make our cosmos a reality. After all of that take a quick test to see how well you know the periodic table.

eARth - ARKit Earth App

This big blue ball that we all live in, get to see the locations of all the countries and the information about a particular country. After spinning around, you can try out some country trivia and see just how well you know the world.

ARKit Anatomy App

Get to walk around a digital cadaver and peek through the human body to see the internal workings of the various systems.

Earth AR App for Android

By using a regular Android phone, you can play around on this table top game where you can play the African map continent country, capital or flag game and test how well you know the continent.

Math AR App

An engaging way of having young students interact with numbers as well as learn how to add or subtract, we took it a step further and added some Kenyan languages so the app can be used even in the most rural areas as well.
